📖 How this works

🎯

No scores, no rankings

There's no visible leaderboard here on purpose. Behind the scenes, the app keeps a light internal number just to help pick fair, balanced teams each round — but nobody sees a score, a rank, or a "bottom of the list." What you'll see next to your name is just a quick word of encouragement based on how your last game went.

🪜

The ladder: Court A vs Court B

Court A is the top court, Court B is the challenger court. After every game, the losing team on Court A drops down to Court B, and the winning team on Court B moves up to Court A. Everyone else stays put. The whole point is that nobody's stuck in a blowout, and nobody's stuck in a hopeless mismatch — the courts quietly recalibrate so every game stays genuinely competitive for everyone on it.

🤝

How teams get paired

Within each court, the 4 players are ranked internally (never shown to anyone). The strongest and weakest team up against the two in the middle — that keeps teams roughly even instead of stacking all the best players together. If that exact pairing just happened, it's automatically reshuffled so you're not stuck with the identical matchup twice in a row. All of this runs quietly in the background specifically so every game feels like a real, close contest — not to rank anyone.

🏆

The final game

For the very last game, the ladder logic is set aside for one moment: today's top 4 are placed on Court A together, no matter which court they were on or who they've already played. Best plays best, one time, to settle it.

🔄

Live sync

Every score you submit saves instantly and shows up on everyone else's phone using the same party code, usually within a few seconds. The little badge in the top corner tells you if your device is connected.

🎙️

Game commentary

After each game, you'll get a quick reaction line — calling out a blowout, a nail-biter, an upset, or a big final-game finish. It's a scripted picker, not a live AI writing fresh text — no account or cost needed, and it still works offline.

🟢

Who serves first

Each game, one player on the court gets a little green dot — that's who serves first. It's picked automatically, favoring whoever's served first the least so far, so the honor rotates fairly across everyone over the whole session instead of always falling to the same person.
