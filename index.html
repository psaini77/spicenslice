📖 How this works

🎯

Party Points

Everyone starts at 0. Win a game and you gain points; lose one and you drop a few. The margin matters a little too — a convincing win earns a bit more, and a close loss costs a bit less. It's not trying to be a precise ranking system — just enough to sort out who's playing well today.

🪜

The ladder: Court A vs Court B

Court A is the top court, Court B is the challenger court. After every game, the losing team on Court A drops down to Court B, and the winning team on Court B moves up to Court A. Everyone else stays put. Play well and you climb toward Court A; a rough game and you slide back — so matches stay close and nobody's stuck in a blowout all night.

🤝

How teams get paired

Within each court, the 4 players are ranked by their current points. The strongest and weakest team up against the two in the middle — that keeps teams roughly balanced instead of stacking all the best players together. If that exact pairing just happened, it's automatically reshuffled so you're not stuck with the identical matchup twice in a row.

🏆

The final game

For the very last game, the ladder logic is set aside for one moment: the top 4 players by points are placed on Court A together, no matter which court they were on or who they've already played. Best plays best, one time, to settle it.

🔄

Live sync

Every score you submit saves instantly and shows up on everyone else's phone using the same party code, usually within a few seconds. The little badge in the top corner tells you if your device is connected.

🎙️

Game commentary

After each game, you'll get a quick reaction line — calling out a blowout, a nail-biter, an upset, or a big final-game finish. It's a scripted picker, not a live AI writing fresh text — no account or cost needed, and it still works offline.
